Donating Furniture Items

We are always in need of items, especially for small apartments. Donations can be brought to us from noon to 2:30 PM every Sunday at 24 Commerce Street, Springfield, New Jersey, 07081. No appointment needed! Check the acceptable items list below for what to donate. If Furniture Assist is too far from your home, please consult our list of other donation organizations.

To have furniture picked up from your home, we recommend the following organizations:

  • College Hunks Hauling Junk
  • Holte and Associates
  • Map Trucking, Ltd.
  • 1-800-Got-Junk
Once you bring us items, you will need to fill out a donation form online or in person.

Acceptable Items

We Accept We Do Not Accept
Living, dining, and bedroom furniture Excessively large, soiled, damaged, or broken furniture
Unstained mattresses and box springs King size mattresses, or mattresses with stains
Toys, dolls, and games Incomplete games or dirty/broken toys/dolls
Linens, including stained linens Window treatments for large windows
Small kitchen/household appliances Tube TVs, excessively large appliances, or appliances with heat coils
Clothes and shoes Clothes and shoes that are ripped or excessively worn
Children's books and cook books Other books, magazines, or newspapers
Baby items and clothes Drop side cribs, car seats, or items without modern safety features
Dishes, glasses, pots, pans, and utensils Chipped dishes, worn pots/pans (incomplete sets are fine)
Lamps, framed mirrors, pictures Mirrors without frames, non-functional lamps
DVD players, game systems (with games) Obsolete electronics (VCRs, 8-track tapes, typewriters)
Holiday items Holiday items for holidays that are more than 3 months away
Decorative items Perfumes, beauty items, medicines, food, paints, or flammables
Area rugs with bound edges Installed carpets without bound edges

We also do not accept pianos/organs, power tools, medical items, hospital beds, stair climbers, medicines, or any items that are dangerous to operate. As a general rule, do not bring anything that has lost its usefulness.
